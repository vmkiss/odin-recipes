Tartiflette

Description

Originating from the mountainous Savoie region of France, this rich, creamy baked casserole made with reblochon cheese, potatoes, bacon, and onions is sure to warm you up on a cold winter's day.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz lardons or diced thick-cut bacon
  • 2 medium yellow onions, diced (about 2 1/2 cups)
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp finely chopped thyme leaves
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • 2 lbs Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3/4 cup dry white wine
  • 8 to 10 oz Reblochon cheese (can subsitute Camembert or Brie), cut into slices or wedges with rind on
  • 1/2 cup creme fraiche
  • Kosher salt and black pepper

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 400 degrees. Add bacon or lardons to a cold skillet and place the plan over medium heat.
  2. Let lardons or bacon cook until begins to render and the edges turn golden (about 7 to 10 minutes).
  3. Add onions to the pan and raise the heat to medium-high. Cook, stirring occasionally, until golden and soft (about 10 more minutes).
  4. Stir in garlic, thyme, and nutmeg, and cook for another minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add potatoes, wine, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/2 tsp pepper, and toss until well-combined.
  6. Cover the pan and cook until potatoes are just tender and the liquid has mostly evaporated (20-25 minutes).
  7. Stir creme fraiche into potatoes, then nestle the cheese in evenly.
  8. Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake, uncovered, until the cheese melts and the potatoes are very tender (20-25 minutes). Serve hot.
