Galettes

Description

This buckwheat crepe is the savory cousin of the sweet crepe that many Americans are familiar with. A favorite in rainy Bretagne, the galette's unique nutty flavor comes from buckwheat flour. While you are free to choose any topping of your liking. the classic - and best, in our opinion - preparation is with ham, gruyere cheese, and a sunny side up egg on top. Best enjoyed with some hard cider.

Ingredients

Galette Batter

  • 2 cups buckwheat flour
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 4 1/2 cups water
  • 1 egg

Additional Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter
  • Ham
  • Eggs
  • Gruyere cheese, grated

Steps

  1. To make the batter: Mix the ingredients together until well-combined. Let the batter sit in the frige for 2 hours or overnight.
  2. Heat an 11 inch cast-iron crepe griddle pan on medium heat until evenly hot. The pan should be hot enough to immediatley punch holes through the batter when poured.
  3. Melt enough butter to coat the whole pan. Pour 1/2 cup of batter and tilt the pan to cover its whole surface.
  4. Cook approximately 2 1/2 minutes on first side, then flip and cook for another 1 1/2 minutes. Remove the galette from the heat and let cool. Repeat with all of the batter, adding butter to the pan as needed to prevent sticking.
  5. Adding toppings: Melt butter and toss in a cooled galette. Put a slice of ham in the middle, followed by grated gruyere. Meanwhile, in a separate skillet, cook an egg in melted butter. When the egg is nearly cooked, gently place it over the gruyer with the yolk centered and fold over the four edges of the galette to leave only the egg visible.
  6. Cover with a lid and heat for a minute or so until the egg is cooked and the bottom of the galette is crisp. Serve immediately.
